A Few Facts About Where We Live:

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet that supports life. It has a unique combination of land, water, and atmosphere, creating a perfect environment for diverse plants, animals, and humans. About 71% of Earth>’s surface is covered by oceans, which regulate the climate and provide habitats for marine life. The atmosphere protects living organisms by filtering harmful solar rays and maintaining temperature balance. Earth also has a magnetic field that shields it from cosmic radiation. Its rich biodiversity and natural resources make it a remarkable and fragile home for all species.

About The Earth:

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun.
  • It weighs about 5.97 sextillion tons (5.97 × 10²⁴ kilograms).
  • Earth’s diameter is around 12,742 kilometers (7,918 miles).
  • About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by water.
  • The planet has one moon called “the Moon.”
  • Earth’s atmosphere is made mostly of nitrogen and oxygen.
  • It takes 24 hours for Earth to rotate once, causing day and night.
  • Earth orbits the Sun once every 365.25 days, which defines a year.

  1. 🌐 Earth Is the Only Known Planet With Life:

    Earth is the only planet in the universe (so far) known to support life, thanks to its perfect distance from the Sun, water availability, and breathable atmosphere.

  2. 🌊 70% of Earth Is Covered by Water:

    About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by oceans, lakes, and rivers. However, only around 3% of that is freshwater!

  3. 🌍 Earth Has a Powerful Magnetic Field:

    Earth’s magnetic field protects us from harmful solar winds and cosmic radiation. It’s generated by the motion of molten iron in the planet’s outer core.

  4. 🌕 Earth Has One Natural Satellite – the Moon:

    The Moon stabilizes Earth’s tilt and causes ocean tides. It's the fifth largest moon in the solar system.

  5. 🌀 Earth Spins at 1,670 km/h (1,038 mph):

    Earth rotates once every 24 hours, creating day and night. Despite spinning so fast, we don’t feel it due to gravity and the atmosphere moving with us.
